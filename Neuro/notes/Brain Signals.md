## 1. Electrical Signals

### 1. Action Potentials (Spikes)
A rapid change in the polarity of a neuron's membrane potential.
#### Membrane Potential 
The neuron membrane separates : 
- Extracellular fluid (outside)
- Intracellular fluid (inside)
Why? - Because ions are distributed differently: 
- Outside : more positive  *(Mostly Sodium $Na^+$)*
- Inside : more negative  *(Mostly Potassium $K^+$)*
This creates a voltage difference. 
Resting Potential  : $V_m \approx -70mV$ 
#### How an Action Potential Happens? 
##### Step 1 : Resting State 
 $V_m \approx -70mV$ . Inside is negative.
##### Step 2 : Depolarization  
Sodium channels open:  $\text{Na}^+ \rightarrow \text{inside}$ 
Cell becomes more positive 
##### Step 3 :  Peak
  Membrane reaches : $+30mV$
##### Step 4 : Repolarization: 
Potassium leaves : $\text{K}^+ \rightarrow \text{outside}$ 

- A neuron has a resting voltage of about ;; -70 mV
<!--SR:!2026-06-17,11,270-->
- When enough stimulation arrives :- 
	1. Neuron's voltage becomes less negative.
	2. Threshold is reached (around -55 mV).
	3. Ion channels open.
	4. Voltage rapidly rises
	5. Neuron fires a spike.
	6. Voltage returns to its resting value.

#### EEG - 
An **Electroencephalogram (EEG)** is a non-invasive, painless medical test that records the **electrical activity of the brain** using small metal electrodes attached to the scalp. 

EEG measures : Voltage Difference $V_1 - V_2$ between 2 locations 

Example, Suppose :
- Electrode = $PO7$
- Reference = $Cz$
=> EEG Records : $V_(PO7) - V_(CZ)$ 

The Need of Reference electrode : 
- It gives us meaningful voltage measurement as there is a comparison between electrodes 

#### Where does EEG comes from? 
POSTSYNAPTIC POTENTIALS : 
*Temporary changes in the electrical membrane potential of a neuron's postsynaptic membrane (usually the dendrite or cell body) that occur when neurotransmitters bind to receptors after being released by a presynaptic neuron.*

Major Source :-
- Excited PSP (EPSPs)
- Inhibitory PSP  (IPSPs)
Q. Why not from [[#1. Action Potentials (Spikes)]] ? 
Cuz action potentials are very brief, few milliseconds, too small individually 
A single neuron contributes nothing to scalp EEG 

#### What creates EEG?
 When large group of neurons activate together especially **Pyramidal Neuron**
 because :-
 - Parallel Dendrites 
 - Electrical effects add together 
 When 1000s or millions activate synchronously, the summed activity becomes visible on EEG

#### Alpha Waves - one famous EEG rhythm 
**Alpha Oscillation** - one of the strongest and easiest EEG rhythms to observe 
			- Frequency $f$: $8-13 Hz$ 
			- Occurs when :-
				- Eyes closed
				- Relaxed Wakefulness 
			- Disappears when:
				- Eyes open
				- Thinking
				- Calculating
				- Paying attention 
				

#### EEG Electrode Locations 
EEG uses 10-20 system 
Electrodes are placed at position that are 10% or 20% of skull distances.

### 1. Magnetoencephalography (MEG) 
Whenever large group of neurons become active together, they generate : -
- Electrical Fields 
- Magnetic Fields

**Core Idea** : Whenever electric current flows, a magnetic field is generated 
#### What does MEG measure? 
Measures magnetic fields generated by neuronal currents 

#### Why MEG work? 
Individual neurons produce tiny magnetic fields 
But millions of aligned neurons produce fields large enough to detect outside the skull

# Artifacts (Noise) 
Signals that do not come from the brain 
## Types of Artifacts 
1. **Physiological Artifacts** 
		Generated by the body
		E.g.. Eye blinking makes large electrical signals
		E.g., Muscle activity like jaw clenching or teeth squeezing 
2. **Non-Physiological Artifacts** 
		Generated by external devices 
		E.g., cell phones, electrical equipment

 Before analysis, Researchers remove artifacts through pre-processing

# Signal Amplification 
**Signal amplification** is the process of increasing the strength (power, voltage, or current) of a signal without significantly altering its original form. 

#### Why is it required? 
Brain signals become weak while passing through : brain tissues, Cerebrospinal fluid, skull, scalp 
Therefore amplification is required.
